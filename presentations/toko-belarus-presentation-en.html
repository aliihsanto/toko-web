Market Context

The Belarus Trade Challenge

Due to international sanctions, Belarus faces significant challenges maintaining vital trade links with European and global markets — disrupting supply chains and creating costly barriers for business growth.

Current Obstacles

  • Restricted direct trade with EU member states
  • Disrupted supply chains for industrial goods
  • Limited access to European technology & machinery
  • Banking and financial transaction barriers
  • Export market limitations for Belarusian firms
  • Complex documentation & compliance requirements
  • Rising costs due to indirect routing

Business Impact

  • Increased costs from longer, indirect supply routes
  • Delays in critical raw material procurement
  • Loss of established European distribution networks
  • Difficulty sourcing replacement parts & technology
  • Reduced competitiveness on global markets
  • Currency exchange and payment processing challenges
  • Inventory management disruptions

Turkey: A Strategic Bridge

Turkey maintains robust trade relations with both Europe and the CIS region. As a NATO member with deep Eurasian ties, Turkey offers a unique, fully compliant corridor for international trade.

18 active free trade zones provide duty-free storage, processing, and re-export capabilities — an ideal hub for Belarus-Europe trade flows.

Turkey–Belarus bilateral trade volume exceeds $1.2 billion annually
