## Going Global with Amazon

Amazon is the world's largest e-commerce platform, with 2.7 billion monthly visitors worldwide. Operating active marketplaces in 21 countries, Amazon offers Turkish exporters one of the most effective ways to reach global customers. In 2025, third-party sales on Amazon exceeded $400 billion, creating unprecedented opportunities for independent sellers.

The presence of Turkish sellers on Amazon is growing exponentially each year. Turkish products see particularly high demand in the textile, food, cosmetics, and home decor categories. In this guide, we will cover every stage of exporting through Amazon in detail.

> Third-party sellers account for more than **60 percent** of total sales on Amazon. This ratio clearly demonstrates the importance of independent sellers on the platform. Turkish exporters still capture only a very small share of this massive market.

## Amazon FBA: What Is Fulfillment by Amazon?

FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) is Amazon's comprehensive logistics service for sellers. Under this program, you ship your products to Amazon's warehouses; Amazon handles packaging, shipping, and customer service.

### Advantages of FBA

**Prime badge**: Products using FBA are automatically enrolled in the Amazon Prime program. This offers free and fast shipping, increasing conversion rates by an average of 30–50 percent.

**Buy Box advantage**: Your likelihood of winning the "Add to Cart" button on Amazon's product page significantly increases with FBA. The Buy Box is where 82 percent of sales take place.

**Customer trust**: Amazon's brand reliability enables even new and unknown brands to make sales. Customers trust products shipped by Amazon logistics more.

**Multi-channel fulfillment**: Amazon warehouses can be used not only for Amazon orders but also for orders from your own website or other platforms.

### FBA Costs

Understanding FBA's cost structure correctly is critical for profitability calculations:

- **Fulfillment fee**: $3–8 per unit depending on product size and weight
- **Storage fee**: $0.75–2.40 per cubic foot per month (seasonal variation)
- **Long-term storage fee**: Additional charges apply for inventory exceeding 365 days
- **Removal and disposal fees**: $0.25–1 per unit for removing unsold products from the warehouse

### FBA vs. FBM (Merchant Fulfilled)

FBM is the model where orders are fulfilled directly by the seller. In some situations, FBM may be preferable:

- **Large or heavy products**: When FBA storage costs would be too high
- **Low-margin products**: When FBA fees erode the profit margin
- **Customized products**: For products requiring per-order customization
- **Local delivery advantage**: When you have your own warehouse in the target market

## Amazon Store Setup: Step by Step

### 1. Account Creation

Open a Professional Seller account through Amazon Seller Central. For a monthly subscription fee of $39.99, you gain unlimited product listings and access to advanced seller tools. Turkish sellers typically start with the Amazon.com (US), Amazon.de (Germany), or Amazon.co.uk (UK) marketplaces.

Documents required to open an account:

- Valid ID or passport
- Company registration documents
- International bank account or Payoneer account
- Tax information (W-8BEN form)
- Credit or debit card

### 2. Product Research and Selection

Choosing the right product is the cornerstone of success on Amazon. Consider these criteria in your product research:

- **Demand**: Products with a monthly sales potential of at least 300–500 units
- **Competition**: Categories that are not excessively competitive and have not yet reached saturation
- **Profit margin**: A minimum 25–30 percent profit margin after FBA fees and Amazon commission
- **Size and weight**: Standard-sized, lightweight products reduce FBA costs
- **Seasonality**: Year-round demand products should be preferred over seasonal ones

Tools like **Jungle Scout**, **Helium 10**, and **AMZScout** are used for product research, competitor analysis, and tracking market trends.

### 3. Product Listing Optimization

A significant portion of sales success on Amazon depends on effective product listings:

**Title optimization**: Create titles of 150–200 characters that include the main keyword and highlight the product's key features.

**Bullet Points**: In the five bullet points, emphasize the product's benefits, features, and differentiators.

**Product description**: Prepare visually rich, detailed product descriptions using A+ Content (Enhanced Brand Content). This content type can increase conversion rates by 5–10 percent.

**Images**: Use high-resolution, white-background main images and lifestyle images. Fill all 7 of Amazon's image slots. Include infographics and size comparison visuals.

**Backend keywords**: Strategically use the 250-byte keyword field that does not appear in the listing but influences the search algorithm.

### 4. Pricing Strategy

Competitive pricing is the key to winning the Buy Box and increasing sales volume:

- **Launch price**: Set prices 10–15 percent below competitors when entering the market to generate initial sales and reviews
- **Dynamic pricing**: Optimize prices in real time using Amazon's automated pricing tool or third-party repricers
- **Bundles and multi-packs**: Create multi-packs instead of single items to increase per-unit profitability

## Amazon Advertising Strategies

Amazon PPC (Pay Per Click) advertising is the fastest way to increase product visibility and boost organic rankings.

### Sponsored Products

The most fundamental and effective ad type. It appears in search results and on product pages. Start with automatic targeting campaigns to identify which keywords drive conversions, then optimize with manual campaigns.

### Sponsored Brands

Used to increase brand awareness. Your brand logo, custom headline, and multiple products are displayed at the top of search results. Available to sellers registered in Brand Registry.

### Sponsored Display

Enables you to run display ads both on and off Amazon. Retargeting campaigns allow you to re-engage customers who viewed your products but did not purchase.

### Ad Budget Management

- Allocate a starting budget of $20–50 per day
- Aim to keep ACoS (Advertising Cost of Sales) in the 25–35 percent range
- Track TACoS (Total ACoS) to evaluate advertising spend relative to total sales
- View advertising spend as an investment during the first 3–6 months; as organic rankings rise, ad dependency decreases

## Success Stories: Inspiration from Turkish Brands

Many Turkish brands have achieved significant success on Amazon. Turkish products stand out particularly in these categories:

**Turkish coffee and food products**: Traditional Turkish coffee, Turkish delight, baklava, and spice varieties achieve high ratings and sales on Amazon. Turkish brands rank among the top in the Turkish coffee category on Amazon.com.

**Home textiles**: Turkish towels, especially peshtemals and cotton towels, see strong demand in the US and European markets. The search term "Turkish towel" is queried tens of thousands of times monthly.

**Natural cosmetics**: Natural cosmetic products such as olive oil-based soaps, rose water, and argan oil benefit from the healthy living trend.

**Copperware and handicrafts**: Turkish copperware, ceramics, and traditional handicrafts sell at premium prices in niche markets.

## Common Mistakes and Solutions

### 1. Insufficient Market Research

Many sellers make product selection based on emotional decisions or insufficient data. Solution: Use data-driven product research tools and conduct a comprehensive analysis process lasting at least 3–4 weeks.

### 2. Low-Quality Images

On Amazon, images are one of the most decisive factors in sales. Invest in professional photography, use A+ Content, and add video content.

### 3. Inventory Management Errors

Stockouts seriously damage organic rankings. Monitor sales velocity, maintain adequate safety stock, and strengthen your supply chain. Regularly check Amazon's Inventory Performance Index (IPI).

### 4. Neglecting Customer Reviews

Reviews play a decisive role in Amazon's search algorithm and customer decisions. Send post-sale follow-up emails, respond professionally to negative reviews, and continuously improve product quality.

### 5. Ignoring Brand Protection

Registering with Amazon Brand Registry provides brand protection and access to advanced seller tools. Protect your brand by securing trademark registration.

### 6. Single-Market Dependency

Focusing solely on Amazon.com limits growth potential. Explore European markets (Germany, UK, France, Italy, Spain) and newly opened markets. Amazon's Unified Account structure allows you to manage European markets from a single account.

## Tax and Legal Obligations

When selling internationally through Amazon, attention to tax compliance is mandatory:

- **US sales tax**: Obligation to collect sales tax in states where nexus exists (Amazon handles this)
- **EU VAT**: Managing EU-wide VAT obligations from a single country via the OSS (One-Stop Shop) system
- **Customs duties**: Pre-calculate applicable tariff rates for imported products
- **Income tax**: Amazon earnings must be included in your Turkish tax return

## Conclusion: The Path to Amazon Success

Exporting through Amazon is one of the most effective ways for Turkish manufacturers and brands to access global markets. Building a sustainable business model on Amazon is achievable with the right product selection, professional listing, strategic ad management, and robust logistics infrastructure.

The key to success is patience and continuous optimization. Making your first sale on Amazon may be easy; however, building a profitable and scalable business requires disciplined work and data-driven decision-making. When Turkey's production capacity and quality products meet Amazon's massive customer base, the resulting potential is truly enormous.
