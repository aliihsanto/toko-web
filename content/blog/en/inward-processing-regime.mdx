## What Is the Inward Processing Regime?

The Inward Processing Regime (IPR) is one of the most important customs regimes applied in Turkey to boost exports and strengthen international competitiveness. This regime provides **exemption from customs duties, VAT, and other fiscal obligations** on the importation of raw materials, semi-finished products, and packaging materials used in the manufacture of products destined for export.

The fundamental logic of the regime is straightforward: inputs imported into Turkey are processed into value-added products, and the resulting goods are exported. The government foregoes import duties during this process, providing exporters with a significant cost advantage.

According to 2025 data, approximately **40%** of Turkey's total exports were carried out under the inward processing regime. This ratio clearly demonstrates the regime's critical importance in Turkish foreign trade.

> The inward processing regime is the most powerful tax optimization tool for firms engaged in export-oriented manufacturing. However, its strict rules and commitment closure obligations make professional management essential.

## Legal Framework

### Legislative Basis

The inward processing regime is governed by the following legislation:

- **Customs Law No. 4458**: Articles 108–122 (fundamental regime provisions)
- **Council of Ministers Decision No. 2005/8391 on Inward Processing Regime**: General framework of the regime
- **Communique No. 2006/12 on Inward Processing Regime**: Implementation principles
- **Circulars and directives**: Current regulations issued by the Ministry of Trade

### Methods of Application

The IPR is applied in two different forms:

**1. Conditional Exemption System (Suspension)**

The most widely used method. Under this system:

- Import duties are suspended (not paid)
- A guarantee is deposited (100% of the duty amount, or reduced for Authorized Economic Operator status holders)
- The guarantee is returned after the processed product is exported
- If exports are not completed within the commitment period, the suspended duties are collected

**2. Drawback System**

Under this system:

- All duties are paid normally at the time of import
- Duties paid are refunded after the processed product is exported
- The refund application is made by documenting that the export has taken place
- Less preferred in practice as it negatively impacts cash flow

## Application Process

### Who Can Apply?

Firms meeting the following conditions may apply for an IPR certificate:

- Legal entity (company) registered in Turkey
- Prior export experience or demonstrated export capacity
- No outstanding tax or social security debts
- No conviction for smuggling offenses
- Ownership of a production facility or an existing contract manufacturing agreement

### Application Documents

Documents required for an inward processing authorization certificate application:

1. **Application form**: Filed electronically with the Ministry of Trade, Directorate General of Exports
2. **Capacity report**: Chamber of industry document demonstrating production capacity
3. **Export commitment project**: Product to be manufactured, raw materials to be used, waste ratios, and export plan
4. **Raw material-product conversion table (consumption table)**: Quantities of raw materials required per unit of finished product
5. **Exporters' association membership certificate**: Registration with the relevant exporters' association
6. **Signature circular**: Authorized signatories
7. **Tax clearance certificate**: Current document from the tax office
8. **Social security clearance certificate**: Current document from the Social Security Institution

### Evaluation and Approval

The application process operates through the following steps:

1. The application is evaluated by the Ministry of Trade, Directorate General of Exports
2. Technical review: The consumption table and production capacity are assessed
3. The opinion of the relevant exporters' association is obtained
4. Approved applications are issued an **Inward Processing Authorization Certificate (IPAC)**
5. The certificate is typically finalized within **15–30 business days** of application

## Information Contained in the IPAC

The Inward Processing Authorization Certificate contains the following information:

- **Certificate holder details**: Company name, address, tax ID number
- **Import list**: Raw materials to be imported, HS codes, quantities, and CIF values
- **Export list**: Finished products to be exported, HS codes, quantities, and FOB values
- **Consumption table**: Raw material-to-product conversion ratios
- **Certificate duration**: Period within which import and export commitments must be closed (typically 12 months)
- **Special conditions**: Additional conditions and limitations, if any

## Tax Exemptions

Tax exemptions provided under the IPR are extremely comprehensive:

### Exempt Taxes

- **Customs duty**: No customs duty is paid on raw material imports
- **Value Added Tax (VAT)**: No VAT is paid on imports
- **Special Consumption Tax (SCT)**: SCT is exempt if applicable
- **Anti-dumping duty**: Exempt if applicable
- **Countervailing duty**: Exempt if applicable
- **Additional financial obligation**: Exemption from quota and surveillance measures
- **Resource Utilization Support Fund (RUSF)**: Exemption from the fund charged on deferred payment imports

### Advantages for Domestic Procurement

Advantages also exist for inputs sourced domestically under the IPR:

- **VAT deferral-offset** mechanism for domestic purchases
- Ability to make domestic purchases under the certificate
- Domestic suppliers can issue VAT-exempt invoices to IPR certificate holders

## Commitment Closure Process

### Closure Obligations

The IPAC holder must fulfill the following obligations by the end of the certificate period:

1. **Export commitment closure**: Documentation that exports of the specified quantity and value have been completed
2. **Import-export balance**: Proof that all imported raw materials were used in the exported finished products
3. **Compliance with the consumption table**: Actual production consumption must be consistent with the ratios specified in the certificate
4. **Certificated and non-certificated export ratios**: Compliance with specified ratios where required

### Closure Documents

Documents required for commitment closure:

- Export customs declarations
- Foreign exchange purchase certificates
- Invoices
- Import customs entry declarations
- Production reports
- Waste and loss reports (if necessary)
- Capacity utilization data

### Closure Deadlines

- The certificate period is typically **12 months**
- Extensions of **up to 6 months** may be granted with justified cause
- Additional time may be requested in cases of force majeure (natural disaster, war, pandemic, etc.)
- The closure process must be completed within **3 months** of the certificate period's expiration

## Waste and Loss Ratios

### The Concept of Waste

The unavoidable loss of raw materials during the production process is known as **waste**. Accepted waste ratios under the IPR are determined by sector and product:

- **Textiles**: 5–15% (fabric cutting waste)
- **Leather**: 15–30% (natural material losses)
- **Metalworking**: 3–10% (machining losses)
- **Food processing**: 5–20% (processing and packaging losses)
- **Chemicals**: 2–5% (reaction and evaporation losses)

### Secondary Processed Products

Residues, shavings, and scraps arising from the production process are considered **secondary processed products**. The status of these products:

- Customs duty is paid if released into free circulation
- No duty is payable if exported
- If destroyed, a destruction report is prepared under customs authority supervision
- Residues with no commercial value may be disposed of upon notification to the customs authority

## Penalties

Failure to meet obligations under the IPR results in serious penalties:

### Commitment Violation Penalties

- **Failure to close export commitment**: Collection of all exempted taxes with **late interest**
- **Partial closure**: Collection of taxes and interest corresponding to the unclosed portion
- **Deadline expiration**: Tax and penalty application if the certificate period expires without an extension

### Administrative Fines

- Using goods imported under the certificate for purposes outside its scope: Fine of **twice the customs duty** amount
- Sale or transfer without certificate coverage: Tax collection + fine
- Preparing misleading documents: Criminal prosecution under Law No. 5607

### Other Sanctions

- Rejection of the firm's IPR certificate applications for a specified period
- Revocation of Authorized Economic Operator status
- Exporters' association sanctions

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Textile Sector

A garment manufacturer exports men's shirts to the EU:

- **Import under IPAC**: 10,000 meters of Italian fabric (CIF $50,000, customs duty 8%)
- **Exempt taxes**: Customs duty $4,000 + VAT $10,800 = **$14,800 savings**
- **Production**: 5,000 shirts manufactured
- **Export**: FOB $100,000 in exports
- **Waste**: 12% fabric cutting waste (within the accepted ratio)

### Example 2: Automotive Parts

An automotive spare parts manufacturer:

- **Import under IPAC**: Aluminum ingots and special alloys (CIF $200,000, customs duty 6%)
- **Exempt taxes**: Customs duty $12,000 + VAT $42,400 + RUSF $6,000 = **$60,400 savings**
- **Production**: Engine components and chassis parts
- **Export**: FOB $500,000 in exports to the EU and the US
- **Waste**: 7% machining waste

### Example 3: Food Sector

A biscuit manufacturer:

- **Import under IPAC**: Cocoa butter, hazelnuts, and specialized packaging material (CIF $80,000)
- **Exempt taxes**: Customs duty + VAT + additional agricultural duty = **approximately $28,000 in total savings**
- **Production**: Chocolate-coated biscuits
- **Export**: To Middle Eastern and African markets
- **Waste**: 8% production waste

## Tips for Successful IPR Management

1. **Prepare the consumption table realistically**: Exaggerated or understated waste ratios create problems
2. **Plan the certificate period correctly**: Account for production and export timelines
3. **Establish a regular tracking system**: Continuously monitor the import-export balance
4. **Revise the certificate on time**: Update the certificate in response to changing conditions
5. **Obtain professional support**: Work in coordination with your customs broker and exporters' association
6. **Prepare closure documents early**: Do not leave them until the last minute
7. **Maintain meticulous waste records**: Retain production reports through certificate closure
8. **Remember the compensating duty**: When exporting to the EU, a compensating duty may need to be paid for inputs originating from third countries
