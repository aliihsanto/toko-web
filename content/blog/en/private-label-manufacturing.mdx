## What Is Private Label Manufacturing?

Private label manufacturing is a business model in which a company sells products produced by another manufacturer under its own brand name. This model is growing rapidly in the global retail sector. **20–25%** of worldwide retail sales consist of private label products, and this share increases every year.

Turkey is a globally preferred hub for private label manufacturing. Key reasons include **flexible production capacity**, **low MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)**, **proximity to Europe**, **Customs Union advantage**, and **high quality standards**.

> Private label production in Turkey can be completed from design to shipment in **6–10 weeks**, with MOQs starting as low as 100 pieces. This flexibility provides a significant advantage, particularly for new brands and boutique retailers.

## Why Private Label Manufacturing in Turkey?

### Advantages

**1. Low Minimum Order Quantities**

While MOQs in Far Eastern countries typically range from 3,000 to 10,000 pieces, many manufacturers in Turkey work with quantities as low as **100–500 pieces**. This enables new brands to start production with low risk.

| Region | Typical MOQ | Lead Time |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| China | 3,000–10,000 pieces | 45–90 days |
| Bangladesh | 5,000–15,000 pieces | 60–120 days |
| **Turkey** | **100–500 pieces** | **21–45 days** |
| Vietnam | 2,000–5,000 pieces | 45–75 days |
| India | 1,000–3,000 pieces | 30–60 days |

**2. Fast Delivery**

Turkey's geographic proximity to Europe offers delivery times of **3–5 days** by road and **5–10 days** by sea. Compared to 30–45 days by sea from the Far East, delivery from Turkey is significantly shorter.

**3. Quality and Flexibility**

- Production meeting European quality standards
- Rapid adaptation to design changes
- No compromise on quality even in small batches
- Detailed communication and transparent process management

**4. Customs Union Advantage**

Thanks to the Customs Union agreement with the EU, textile products manufactured in Turkey can be exported to EU countries at **0% customs duty**. This provides a significant advantage in price competition.

**5. Fully Integrated Production**

In Turkey, the entire production chain — from yarn to fabric, dyeing to garment making — is accessible within a single geography. This integrated structure positively impacts quality control and production speed.

## Step-by-Step Private Label Production Process

### Step 1: Concept and Planning (1–2 Weeks)

The first stage of the production process is clearly defining the brand concept and product planning.

**Items to determine:**
- **Target market**: Which country/region, which age group?
- **Product category**: T-shirts, shirts, denim, sportswear?
- **Price segment**: Economy, mid-range, premium?
- **Collection size**: How many styles, how many colors?
- **Size range**: XS–3XL, standard or plus size?
- **Estimated quantity**: Total pieces for the initial order
- **Budget**: Total investment plan

### Step 2: Supplier Research and Selection (2–3 Weeks)

**Supplier sourcing channels:**

- **Trade fairs**: Istanbul Textile Fair, Texhibition, Fashion Prime
- **Online platforms**: Alibaba, Maker's Row, Kompass
- **Trade associations**: ITKIB (Istanbul Textile and Apparel Exporters' Associations)
- **Professional sourcing agencies**: Sourcing firms like Toko Trading
- **Referrals**: Industry connections and networks

**Supplier evaluation criteria:**

1. **Production capacity**: Monthly capacity, MOQ, size range
2. **Specialization**: Womenswear, menswear, children's, sportswear
3. **Quality certifications**: OEKO-TEX, GOTS, ISO 9001, BSCI
4. **Reference customers**: Existing brand portfolio
5. **Infrastructure**: Factory size, machinery, R&D
6. **Communication**: Language skills, response speed, professionalism
7. **Financial reliability**: Company history, trade registry

> Before placing the first order, always conduct a **factory visit** or have a trusted representative visit the factory. Physical assessment is the most reliable method in supplier selection.

### Step 3: Design and Pattern Development (2–4 Weeks)

**Design process:**

1. **Mood board preparation**: Inspiration sources, color palette, target look
2. **Technical drawing (Tech Pack)**: Detailed technical specification of the product
3. **Fabric and accessory selection**: Lab-dip, hang tag, label design
4. **Pattern development**: Measurement table and pattern preparation
5. **Proto sample**: First trial product

**Tech Pack essentials:**
- Front and back technical drawings
- Measurement table (all sizes)
- Fabric information (type, weight, color)
- Accessory details (buttons, zippers, labels)
- Sewing details (stitch types, edge finishes)
- Care instructions
- Packaging specifications

### Step 4: Sampling Process (2–4 Weeks)

The sampling process typically consists of several stages:

**Proto Sample (1st Sample):**
- First trial production
- Pattern and form check
- Detail and workmanship evaluation
- Fabric and color conformity

**Fit Sample (2nd Sample):**
- Application of corrections from the proto sample
- Pattern fine-tuning
- Size grading check

**Pre-Production Sample (PP Sample):**
- Final sample ready for mass production
- Complete with all accessories and labels
- Full product including packaging
- **This sample will serve as the production reference**

**Approximate sampling costs:**
- Proto sample: $50–150 per style
- Fit sample: $30–80 per style
- PP sample: $30–80 per style
- Lab-dip (color sample): $20–50 per color

### Step 5: Production Order (1 Week)

Following sample approval, the production order covers:

- **Order form**: Style, size, color, and quantity breakdown
- **Payment terms**: Typically 30% deposit, 70% before shipment
- **Delivery date**: Date determined based on production lead time
- **Quality criteria**: AQL values, tolerances
- **Shipment details**: Trade terms such as FOB, CIF, DDP

### Step 6: Production Process (3–6 Weeks)

**Production stages:**

1. **Fabric procurement and inspection** (1 week)
2. **Cutting** (2–3 days)
3. **Sewing** (1–3 weeks, depending on quantity)
4. **Washing/dyeing** (if applicable, 2–3 days)
5. **Pressing and quality control** (2–3 days)
6. **Labeling and packaging** (1–2 days)
7. **Final inspection** (1 day)

### Step 7: Branding and Labeling

Branding elements in private label production:

- **Main label**: Brand logo, size information
- **Care label**: Washing instructions, fiber content, country of manufacture
- **Hang tag**: Brand story, price tag
- **Packaging**: Polybag, box, tape, sticker
- **Carton box**: Outer packaging, branded or neutral
- **Barcode**: EAN/UPC codes (for retail sales)

**Approximate labeling costs:**

| Label Type | Unit Cost |
|------------|-----------|
| Woven main label | $0.05–0.15 |
| Printed care label | $0.02–0.05 |
| Hang tag | $0.10–0.30 |
| Polybag | $0.03–0.10 |
| Carton box (12 pieces) | $0.50–1.50 |

### Step 8: Quality Control and Inspection

Quality control stages during and after production:

- **Fabric inspection**: Pre-production fabric quality control
- **Inline inspection**: At 20% and 60% of production
- **Final inspection**: AQL-based after production completion
- **Laboratory testing**: When required (color fastness, shrinkage, etc.)

### Step 9: Shipment and Logistics

**Shipping options:**

- **Air freight**: 2–5 days (high cost, urgent orders)
- **Road freight**: 3–7 days (economical for Europe)
- **Sea freight**: 5–15 days (most economical for large volumes)
- **Express courier**: 3–5 days (for small lots via DHL, FedEx, UPS)

## Cost Analysis

### Cost Components

Typical cost breakdown for a T-shirt production:

| Item | Share | Approximate Cost |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Fabric | 40–50% | $2.00–3.50 |
| Labor (sewing) | 20–25% | $1.00–1.80 |
| Accessories | 5–8% | $0.30–0.50 |
| Washing/finishing | 5–8% | $0.25–0.50 |
| Packaging and labels | 3–5% | $0.20–0.40 |
| Overhead | 10–15% | $0.50–1.00 |
| **Total FOB** | **100%** | **$4.25–7.70** |

### Factors Affecting Price

- **Order quantity**: **15–25%** price difference between 100 pieces vs. 1,000 pieces
- **Fabric quality**: Organic cotton is **30–50%** more expensive than standard cotton
- **Print/embroidery**: **$0.30–2.00** additional cost depending on print type and number of colors
- **Wash effects**: Processes like stone wash and enzyme wash add **$0.50–1.50**
- **Certifications**: GOTS, OEKO-TEX certified production adds **10–20%** extra cost

## Common Mistakes and Solutions

### Mistake 1: Insufficient Tech Pack
**Problem**: An incomplete or ambiguous technical specification extends the sampling process and leads to misunderstandings.
**Solution**: Prepare a detailed tech pack or seek professional assistance.

### Mistake 2: Single Supplier Dependency
**Problem**: Working with a single supplier creates concentrated risk.
**Solution**: Develop relationships with at least 2–3 alternative suppliers.

### Mistake 3: Skipping the Sampling Process
**Problem**: Skipping the sampling stage to save time leads to major problems in mass production.
**Solution**: Allow sufficient time for the sampling process; do not proceed to production without a PP sample.

### Mistake 4: Communication Gaps
**Problem**: Inadequate communication during the production process leads to expectation mismatches.
**Solution**: Hold weekly update meetings and implement written approval processes.

### Mistake 5: Cost-Only Focus
**Problem**: Focusing solely on price can result in quality compromises.
**Solution**: Evaluate total cost (quality issues, returns, brand value).

## Legal Considerations

### Intellectual Property Protection

- **Trademark registration**: Register your trademark in Turkey and target markets
- **NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)**: Sign a confidentiality agreement with the supplier
- **Design protection**: Register your original designs
- **Contract**: Prepare a detailed production contract

### Regulations to Comply With

- **EU labeling regulations**: Fiber content, care instructions, country of manufacture
- **REACH regulation**: Chemical restrictions
- **CPSIA** (US market): Children's product safety standard
- **Customs tariffs**: HS codes and preferential tariff applications
