## The Customs Union: Turkey's Strongest Trade Card

The Customs Union agreement between Turkey and the European Union has been in force since 1996 and represents a strategic partnership that has dramatically increased the trade volume for both sides. This agreement directly connects Turkey to the world's largest single market and offers importers unparalleled advantages.

In 2025, Turkey-EU bilateral trade volume exceeded **$190 billion**. While the EU is Turkey's largest trading partner, Turkey is also the EU's sixth-largest trade partner. The structural advantages provided by the Customs Union form the foundation of this relationship.

> The Customs Union has made Turkey **a natural extension of the EU's internal market**. This provides importers sourcing from Turkey with advantages that those sourcing from the Far East or other third countries can never access.

## 1. Zero Customs Duties: Direct Cost Advantage

The most tangible and measurable advantage of the Customs Union is the **zero customs duty on industrial products**. Industrial goods and processed agricultural products exported from Turkey to EU countries can enter the EU market without being subject to any customs duties.

**Cost comparison:**

- Average customs duty on imports from China to the EU: **4–14%** (varies by product)
- Average customs duty on imports from India to the EU: **3–12%**
- Customs duty on imports from Turkey to the EU: **0%**

For companies engaged in high-volume trade, this difference translates into savings of **millions of euros** annually. For example, on €10 million worth of textile imports, approximately €1.2 million in customs duties would be payable on Chinese-sourced goods, while this cost is zero for Turkish-sourced goods.

### Product Groups Within Scope

- All industrial products (automotive, textiles, machinery, electronics, chemicals)
- Processed agricultural products (under certain conditions)
- Steel and metal products
- Plastic and rubber products
- Furniture and wood products

## 2. Harmonized Standards: One Certification, Two Markets

Under the Customs Union, Turkey has largely transposed the EU's **technical legislation and standards** into its domestic law. This harmonization provides enormous convenience for importers: a product manufactured and certified in Turkey is also recognized as valid in the EU market.

**Harmonized areas:**

- Machinery safety directives
- Low-voltage equipment
- Electromagnetic compatibility (EMC)
- Pressure equipment
- Personal protective equipment
- Toy safety
- Construction materials
- Medical devices

This means importers are not required to undergo a **separate conformity assessment**. Certificates obtained in Turkey are directly accepted at EU customs.

## 3. CE Marking: Easy Market Access

**CE marking** is a mandatory mark indicating that products meet the EU's essential safety, health, and environmental protection requirements. Since Turkey has harmonized EU legislation, Turkish manufacturers can also apply CE marking.

**Advantages for importers:**

- CE-certified products from Turkey can enter the EU market directly
- No separate testing and certification process is required
- Turkish testing laboratories are recognized by the EU (TÜRKAK accreditation)
- Certification timelines and costs are much lower compared to the Far East

> Sourcing CE-certified products from the Far East typically requires the involvement of a European Notified Body. Sourcing from Turkey eliminates this process, saving both **time** and **money**.

## 4. Supply Chain Speed: The Value of Time

Turkey's geographic proximity to Europe, combined with the customs facilitations provided by the Customs Union, creates an extraordinary speed advantage.

**Delivery time comparison:**

| Source Country | Sea Freight | Road Freight | Air Freight |
|---|---|---|---|
| Turkey | 3–7 days | 2–5 days | 1 day |
| China | 25–40 days | - | 3–5 days |
| India | 18–30 days | - | 3–5 days |
| Vietnam | 28–42 days | - | 4–6 days |

**Customs processing times:**

- From Turkey to the EU: Average **2–4 hours** (simplified procedures thanks to the Customs Union)
- From third countries to the EU: Average **1–3 days** (full customs controls)

This speed difference is critically important, particularly for companies operating **just-in-time** production, the fashion industry, and seasonal products.

## 5. Quality Assurance: Production at European Standards

Turkey's legislative alignment with the EU creates a natural assurance mechanism in terms of production quality. Turkish manufacturers have **over 30 years** of experience in producing to EU standards.

**Quality assurance infrastructure:**

- **TÜRKAK** (Turkish Accreditation Agency): Mutual recognition agreement with EU accreditation bodies
- **TSE** (Turkish Standards Institute): National standards body implementing ISO and EN standards
- **Thousands of ISO 9001-certified facilities**: Turkey ranks among the countries with the most ISO certifications worldwide
- **Sectoral certifications**: International certifications such as GOTS, OEKO-TEX, BRC, IFS, and FSSC 22000 are widely held

## 6. Origin Cumulation: Flexible Sourcing

The Customs Union and the Pan-Euro-Mediterranean Cumulation of Origin System (PEM Convention) offer importers sourcing from Turkey the advantage of **origin cumulation**.

**How does it work?**

Turkish manufacturers can export products made using raw materials sourced from EU countries or other PEM Convention member states, meeting the preferential rules of origin. This enables importers to:

- Access a broader range of raw material sources
- Optimize costs
- Gain flexibility in the supply chain

Origin cumulation provides significant advantages, particularly in the **textiles, automotive, and electronics** sectors, making Turkey an ideal production and assembly hub for complex supply chains.

## 7. Competitive Production Costs

The Customs Union's zero-tariff advantage, combined with Turkey's already competitive production costs, creates a very strong cost-value balance.

**Cost component comparison (EU average = 100):**

- **Labor costs**: Turkey 35–45 (one-third of the EU average)
- **Energy costs**: Turkey 60–70
- **Real estate/rent**: Turkey 25–40
- **Raw materials**: Turkey 80–95 (many raw materials available locally)
- **Logistics (to the EU)**: Turkey 40–50 (half that of the Far East)

This cost structure, combined with the Customs Union's zero-tariff advantage, makes Turkey a sourcing option approximately **40–60% below** production costs in Europe.

## 8. Intellectual Property Protection: A Secure Trade Environment

Turkey has **intellectual property rights legislation aligned with the EU**. This provides a significant source of assurance for importers:

- **Patent protection**: Signatory to the European Patent Convention
- **Trademark registration**: International trademark registration via the Madrid Protocol
- **Design protection**: National legislation aligned with EU design law
- **Copyright**: Signatory to the Berne Convention and WIPO agreements

The problems of **counterfeit products, trademark infringement, and design copying** frequently encountered in Far Eastern sourcing are largely eliminated when sourcing from Turkey. The Turkish legal system applies effective sanctions against intellectual property violations.

## 9. Environmental and Social Compliance

Growing **ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance)** requirements in Europe mandate that importers document the environmental and social compliance of their supply chains. Turkey holds distinct advantages over Far Eastern alternatives in this area:

**Environmental compliance:**

- Production compliant with REACH and RoHS regulations
- Waste management legislation aligned with the EU
- Developing carbon footprint reporting infrastructure
- Increasing renewable energy usage

**Social compliance:**

- Signatory to all ILO conventions
- Occupational health and safety legislation aligned with the EU
- Effective enforcement of child labor prohibition
- Minimum wage and working conditions legislation

> Under the **EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD)**, starting from 2026, large EU companies are required to monitor human rights and environmental violations in their supply chains. Turkey's legislative alignment makes it easier to meet these requirements.

## 10. Customs Union Modernization Perspective

Turkey and the EU continue negotiations on the **modernization and expansion** of the Customs Union. Once the modernization is completed, additional advantages for importers will include:

- Full inclusion of **agricultural products**
- Liberalization of **trade in services**
- Reciprocal access to **public procurement**
- Harmonization of **e-commerce** regulations
- Strengthening of the **dispute resolution mechanism**

This modernization has the potential to increase Turkey-EU trade volume by **$30–40 billion annually** and will open entirely new areas of opportunity for importers.

## Practical Implementation: How to Leverage the Customs Union

### A.TR Movement Certificate

For goods in free circulation from Turkey to enter the EU duty-free, an **A.TR Movement Certificate** must be issued. This document certifies that the goods are in free circulation in Turkey and are exempt from customs duties under the Customs Union.

### EUR.1 Movement Certificate

The **EUR.1 Movement Certificate** is used to benefit from preferential tariff rates on exports to countries with which Turkey has signed Free Trade Agreements (FTAs).

### Approved Exporter Status

Companies with high-volume and regular export activity can obtain **Approved Exporter** status to further expedite customs procedures.
