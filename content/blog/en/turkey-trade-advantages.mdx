## Turkey: Where Three Continents Meet for Business

Few countries in the world can claim the kind of geographic, economic, and strategic advantages that Turkey offers to international traders. Positioned at the literal crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa, Turkey is not merely a bridge between continents -- it is a fully developed industrial economy with world-class infrastructure, a young and hungry workforce, and deep integration into global trade networks.

In 2025, Turkey's merchandise exports hit a record $267 billion, and the country is targeting $280 billion for 2026. It ranks among the top 20 exporting nations globally and among the top 7 in Europe. More than 80,000 Turkish companies are active exporters, shipping goods to over 200 countries and territories. These are not the numbers of an "emerging" economy; these are the numbers of a mature, diversified, and globally competitive trading nation.

> Turkey provides access to markets comprising 1.5 billion consumers within a 4-hour flight radius. This geographic advantage, combined with EU Customs Union membership and over 30 free trade agreements, makes Turkey one of the most strategically valuable sourcing and manufacturing locations in the world.

## Strategic Location: The Centre of the World's Trade Routes

Turkey's geographic position is not just an advantage -- it is the single most important factor in the country's trade success. No other country on earth provides simultaneous access to European, Middle Eastern, Central Asian, North African, and Black Sea markets with such short transit times.

### Gateway to Four Seas

Turkey is bordered by four different seas, giving it unmatched maritime connectivity:

- **Mediterranean Sea**: Direct access to Southern European and North African markets. Mersin port connects to the Suez Canal route, linking Turkey to East Africa and Asia.
- **Black Sea**: Opens trade routes to Russia, Ukraine, Romania, Bulgaria, and Georgia. Trabzon and Samsun ports serve as key gateways to the Caucasus and Central Asia.
- **Aegean Sea**: Connects to Greece and the wider Southern European market. Izmir's Alsancak port is one of the busiest container terminals in the Eastern Mediterranean.
- **Sea of Marmara**: Located between the Bosphorus and Dardanelles straits -- two of the world's most important waterways. Istanbul's Ambarli port, Turkey's largest container terminal, handles over 3.5 million TEU annually.

### Overland Connections to Three Continents

Turkey's road and rail networks connect it to all neighbouring regions:

- **To Europe**: TIR road transport reaches Germany in 4-6 days, Italy in 3-5 days, and the United Kingdom in 6-8 days via Bulgaria and Greece. The Baku-Tbilisi-Kars railway links Turkey to the Middle Corridor, a key route connecting China to Europe via Central Asia.
- **To the Middle East**: Border crossings with Iraq, Iran, and Syria provide road access to the Persian Gulf and the broader Middle East. Delivery to Iraq takes 2-3 days by road.
- **To the Caucasus and Central Asia**: Overland connections through Georgia reach Azerbaijan, Turkmenistan, and beyond. The BTK railway is a critical link in this corridor.
- **To North Africa**: Ferry and ro-ro services from Mediterranean ports connect to Egypt, Tunisia, and Libya.

For industries where speed to market matters -- fashion, seasonal products, perishable goods, just-in-time manufacturing -- this delivery time advantage is transformative. A European retailer can replenish stock from Turkey in under a week, compared to 5-6 weeks from China.

## EU Customs Union: Turkey's Decisive Competitive Edge

The Turkey-EU Customs Union, operational since 1996, is arguably Turkey's single greatest trade advantage. It places Turkey in a category that no other non-EU country occupies: tariff-free access to the world's largest single market of 450 million consumers.

### Zero Customs Duty on Industrial Products

Under the Customs Union, industrial products and processed agricultural products move between Turkey and the EU with zero customs duty. This is not a preferential rate -- it is zero. The proof of eligibility is the ATR Movement Certificate.

**Competitive impact by product example:**

| Product | Turkey to EU (with ATR) | China to EU (MFN rate) | Bangladesh to EU (GSP) | Turkey's Advantage |
|---------|------------------------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Cotton T-shirt | 0% | 12% | 9.6% | 12 percentage points vs. China |
| Ceramic tiles | 0% | 5% | 5% | 5 percentage points |
| Steel rebar | 0% | Up to 7.5% | 7.5% | Up to 7.5 percentage points |
| Automotive parts | 0% | 3-4.5% | 3-4.5% | Up to 4.5 percentage points |

For low-margin products where even 2-3% makes the difference between winning and losing a contract, the Customs Union advantage is decisive.

### Regulatory Harmonisation with the EU

Turkey has transposed vast swathes of EU legislation into domestic law, meaning Turkish-manufactured products are designed and produced to EU standards from the factory floor:

- **CE marking**: Turkish manufacturers are fully experienced in CE conformity assessment. Products carry CE marks that are recognised across all 27 EU member states.
- **REACH compliance**: Turkey's chemical regulations mirror the EU's Registration, Evaluation, Authorisation, and Restriction of Chemicals (REACH) framework.
- **Product safety**: Consumer product safety regulations in Turkey are aligned with EU directives.
- **Food safety**: HACCP, ISO 22000, and EU food hygiene regulations are widely implemented across Turkey's food processing sector.
- **Intellectual property**: Patent, trademark, and copyright protection in Turkey meets EU standards.

This regulatory alignment means that products sourced from Turkey require minimal adaptation to comply with European market requirements -- a significant advantage over suppliers from countries where different standards apply.

## Free Trade Agreements: A Network Spanning Four Continents

Beyond the EU Customs Union, Turkey has built an extensive network of Free Trade Agreements (FTAs) that provide preferential tariff access to markets across Europe, Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

### Active FTAs as of 2026

**Europe**: EFTA (Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Liechtenstein), Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, Serbia, North Macedonia, Kosovo, Georgia, Moldova, Faroe Islands, United Kingdom

**Asia-Pacific**: South Korea, Malaysia, Singapore

**Middle East and North Africa**: Egypt, Tunisia, Morocco, Israel (industrial products), Mauritius

**Americas**: Chile, Colombia, Venezuela

**Under negotiation**: Japan, Indonesia, Thailand, Mexico, Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries

### What This Means for Importers

If you source products from Turkey and export to any of these FTA partner countries, you benefit from reduced or zero customs duties, provided the goods meet the applicable rules of origin and are accompanied by a EUR.1 Movement Certificate. This makes Turkey an ideal platform for companies that serve multiple markets simultaneously.

## Young Workforce: Europe's Demographic Advantage

While Western Europe faces an ageing population crisis, Turkey has one of the youngest and most dynamic workforces on the continent.

**Key demographics:**

- **Total population**: 85.3 million (2026)
- **Median age**: 33.1 years (compare: Germany 45.7, Italy 47.3, Japan 48.6)
- **Working-age population (15-64)**: 58 million
- **University students**: 8+ million enrolled in higher education
- **Annual university graduates**: 900,000+
- **Annual engineering graduates**: 100,000+
- **Multilingual workforce**: English, German, Russian, Arabic, and French are widely spoken in the business community

This young demographic profile translates directly into manufacturing capacity. Turkey has no shortage of workers for factories, and the talent pipeline from universities ensures a steady supply of engineers, designers, and managers for increasingly sophisticated industries.

### Labour Cost Advantage

Turkey offers substantial labour cost savings compared to Western Europe while maintaining productivity levels that far exceed other low-cost manufacturing destinations:

| Cost Factor | Turkey (2026) | Western Europe | Savings |
|-------------|--------------|---------------|---------|
| Monthly minimum wage | ~$650 | ~$2,100 | 69% |
| Average engineer salary | ~$1,500/month | ~$4,500/month | 67% |
| Industrial electricity | ~$0.09/kWh | ~$0.22/kWh | 59% |
| Factory rent | ~$4-8/sqm/month | ~$12-25/sqm/month | 60-70% |

These savings are not at the expense of quality. Turkish workers operate the same machinery, follow the same standards, and produce the same quality as their Western European counterparts -- at a fraction of the cost.

Turkey is not a one-sector economy. Its industrial base spans virtually every manufacturing category, making it a versatile sourcing destination where you can consolidate multiple product needs with suppliers in a single country.

- **Regional airports**: Ankara Esenboga, Izmir Adnan Menderes, Antalya, and Sabiha Gokcen all offer significant cargo handling capacity.

## Investment Incentives: Welcoming Foreign Capital

Turkey actively courts foreign investment through a comprehensive incentive framework:

### Investment Incentive Certificates

- Customs duty exemption on imported machinery and equipment
- VAT exemption on domestic machinery purchases
- Reduced corporate tax rates (depending on region and sector)
- Social security premium support for new employees
- Land allocation in organised industrial zones at favourable terms

### Technology Development Zones (Technoparks)

Turkey operates over 90 active technoparks, offering:
- Corporate tax exemption on income earned from R&D and software development activities
- Income tax exemption for researchers and software developers employed in the zone
- VAT exemption on software and R&D deliverables

### Organised Industrial Zones (OIZs)

With over 350 organised industrial zones offering ready-made infrastructure:
- Roads, electricity, water, natural gas, and waste treatment facilities pre-installed
- Land available at below-market rates
- Tax advantages for investors in priority development regions
- Cluster effects: proximity to other manufacturers in the same sector

### R&D Incentives

- 100% tax deduction on R&D expenditures
- 80% discount on social security premiums for R&D personnel
- Cash grants for approved R&D projects through TUBITAK (Turkey's scientific research council)
- Patent box regime: reduced tax on income derived from patented inventions
