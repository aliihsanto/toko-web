## Turkey's 2026 Export Vision: A Record-Breaking Year

After closing 2025 with an export volume of $267 billion, Turkey has set its sights on a **$285 billion** export target for 2026. This goal represents not merely a numerical increase, but also a fundamental transformation in the country's export structure. Increasing the share of high-value-added products, expanding into new markets, and integrating digital commerce form the core pillars of this transformation.

The Ministry of Trade's 2026–2028 Export Strategy Action Plan aims to strengthen Turkey's position in global value chains and raise the share of medium-high technology products in exports to 40 percent. This vision seeks to transform Turkey from being solely a manufacturing base into a technology- and innovation-driven export powerhouse.

> Turkey is an economic force that exports to **more than 200 countries** and ranks among the top 25 exporting nations worldwide in 2026. This achievement stems from the combination of its diversified production capacity and strategic geographic location.

## Sectoral Export Analysis

### 1. Automotive Sector: The Leading Force

The Turkish automotive sector continued to be Turkey's largest export item in 2025, with **$38.5 billion** in exports. This figure is expected to exceed **$42 billion** in 2026. The sector is gaining new momentum, particularly with the increase in electric vehicle production and TOGG's expansion into international markets.

**Key dynamics:**

- **Electric vehicle production**: Investments in EV production lines at Turkey's automotive factories are beginning to bear fruit in 2026. Electric model production by Stellantis, Ford Otosan, and Hyundai Assan is driving export figures upward.
- **Parts and components exports**: The automotive parts sector constitutes a significant component of the industry with $15 billion in exports. Europe's supply chain diversification strategy is opening new doors for Turkish parts manufacturers.
- **TOGG's international expansion**: Turkey's domestic electric car brand TOGG is entering the European market in 2026, adding a new dimension to the export portfolio.

### 2. Textiles and Apparel: A Sustainable Transformation

The textiles and apparel sector holds the second-largest position with **$22 billion** in exports. The sector's most important trend in 2026 is sustainability and the ability to adapt to fast-fashion cycles.

**Competitive advantages:**

- 3–7 day delivery time to Europe (versus 30–45 days from the Far East)
- Small-batch production flexibility
- Organic and sustainable textile certifications (GOTS, OEKO-TEX)
- Access to high-quality raw materials
- Compliance capacity for the EU Green Deal

### 3. Machinery and Components: A Technological Leap

The machinery sector demonstrated remarkable growth in 2025 with **$26 billion** in exports. The 2026 target has been set at **$29 billion**. The sector is strengthening its global competitiveness, particularly in CNC machines, food processing machinery, packaging equipment, and textile machinery.

**Growth areas:**

- Industry 4.0-compatible smart machines
- Robotics and automation systems
- Agricultural machinery (Africa and Central Asia markets)
- Construction and mining equipment
- Energy equipment (wind turbine components)

### 4. Food and Agricultural Products: Natural Abundance

Turkey's food and agricultural product exports exceeded **$28 billion** in 2025. With a **$31 billion** target for 2026, the sector continues to be one of Turkey's most consistently growing export categories.

**Key product groups:**

- **Hazelnuts**: Turkey accounts for 70 percent of global production and is the world leader in hazelnut exports
- **Dried fruits and pulses**: Exports of apricots, grapes, figs, and pistachios are continuously growing
- **Olive oil**: Turkey is the third-largest exporter of premium-quality olive oil, after Spain and Italy
- **Aquaculture**: Turkey is Europe's largest supplier, particularly in sea bream and sea bass farming
- **Processed foods**: The biscuit, chocolate, pasta, and bakery products sector is growing rapidly

### 5. Chemical Sector: Strategic Growth

Chemical sector exports reached **$24 billion** in 2025 and are targeting **$27 billion** in 2026. Petrochemicals, cosmetics, paint and coatings, plastic raw materials, and pharmaceuticals are the primary growth drivers.

**Strategic advantages:**

- Increased petrochemical capacity through STAR Refinery and Petkim integration
- Biotechnology investments in the pharmaceutical sector
- Rising brand value in cosmetics and personal care products
- Potential to become a regional power in agrochemicals

## Target Markets and Trade Agreements

### European Union: Strategic Partner

The EU is Turkey's largest trading partner, absorbing approximately **42 percent** of Turkey's total exports. Thanks to the Customs Union agreement, zero-tariff advantages for industrial products continue. Negotiations for updating the Customs Union are expected to gain momentum in 2026.

**Emerging opportunities in the EU market:**

- Demand for sustainable products under the Green Deal
- Supply chain diversification trend (nearshoring)
- Demand for digital transformation equipment and services
- Healthcare products and medical devices

### US Market: Growing Potential

Turkey's exports to the US exceeded **$15 billion** in 2025. Growth is expected in 2026, particularly in the defense industry, automotive parts, agricultural products, and textiles. The dollar-based price advantage provides Turkish exporters with a competitive edge.

### Middle East and North Africa (MENA): Proximity Advantage

The MENA region accounts for **20 percent** of Turkey's exports. Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 projects, the UAE's economic diversification strategy, and Iraq's reconstruction present significant opportunities for Turkish exporters.

### Africa: The Market of the Future

Turkey's exports to Africa have tripled over the past 10 years. A **$22 billion** export target has been set for 2026. Nigeria, Kenya, Ethiopia, South Africa, and Egypt are the primary target markets.

## Competitive Advantages and Strategic Positioning

### Geographic Advantage

Turkey provides access to markets with **1.5 billion people** within a 4-hour flight radius, situated at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and Africa. This location reduces logistics costs while shortening delivery times.

### Production Capacity and Flexibility

- A young and dynamic workforce of 85 million people
- More than 50,000 active exporting companies
- Thousands of ISO 9001-certified production facilities
- Flexibility ranging from small-batch production to mass manufacturing

### Trade Agreement Network

Turkey's **more than 30** free trade agreements provide exporters with preferential tariff rates and broad market access. Additionally, bilateral investment protection agreements and double taxation avoidance agreements enhance business security.

## Digital Transformation and E-Exports

The share of digital commerce in exports is growing rapidly in 2026. Under the Ministry of Trade's **E-Export Strategy**:

- **Digital marketplaces**: Increasing the visibility of Turkish products on platforms like Amazon, Alibaba, and Etsy
- **D2C (Direct-to-Consumer)**: Turkish brands selling globally through their own digital channels
- **B2B digital platforms**: The growing role of professional sourcing platforms like Toko Trading
- **Digital payment infrastructure**: Blockchain-based trade finance and smart contracts

> E-exports reached **$6 billion** in 2025, with the 2026 target set at **$8 billion**. This area is opening new export channels, particularly for SMEs.

## Government Incentives and Support Programs

### Ministry of Trade Support

- **Market research support**: Grants of up to 70 percent
- **Trade fair participation support**: Coverage of booth, travel, and accommodation expenses
- **Overseas office support**: Rent subsidies for offices, stores, and warehouses
- **Brand support (Turquality)**: Global brand development program
- **Design support**: Support for product design and R&D expenditures

### Eximbank Financing

- Export credits (rediscount, pre-/post-shipment)
- Export credit insurance
- Investment loans
- Foreign exchange-earning services loans

## 2026 Export Trends and Projections

### Green Exports

The impact of the European Green Deal on trade policies continues to intensify. Under the **Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM)**, carbon footprint reporting is becoming mandatory in the steel, cement, aluminum, fertilizer, and electricity sectors. It is critical for Turkish exporters to be prepared for this transition.

### Nearshoring Trend

Europe's post-pandemic supply chain diversification strategy is making Turkey an ideal hub for **nearshoring**. Efforts to reduce dependence on the Far East are translating into new orders for Turkish manufacturers.

### Halal Economy

The global **halal economy** market is expected to exceed $3 trillion in 2026. Turkey is a significant player in this market with its halal-certified production capacity in food, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, and textiles.

### Defense and Aerospace

Defense and aerospace sector exports reached **$6 billion** in 2025. UAVs, armored vehicles, electronic warfare systems, and simulation technologies are in high demand on the international market. A **$7.5 billion** export target has been set for 2026.

## Conclusion: Seizing the Opportunities of 2026

Turkey's 2026 export potential is being shaped around new technology areas, digital commerce, and sustainable production, in addition to traditionally strong sectors. The structural transformation in global supply chains is making Turkey's strategic position even more valuable.

**Key recommendations for exporters:**

1. **Prioritize sustainability investments** — becoming mandatory for the EU market
2. **Actively leverage digital channels** — e-exports are the fastest-growing segment
3. **Explore new markets** — Africa and Southeast Asia hold significant potential
4. **Maximize government incentives** — Turquality, market research, and trade fair support
5. **Increase value added** — focus on processed products, services, and brand exports rather than raw materials
